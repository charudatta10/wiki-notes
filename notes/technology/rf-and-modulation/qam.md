# [[Quadrature Amplitude Modulation]] ([[QAM]])

[[Quadrature Amplitude Modulation]] ([[QAM]]) is a [[modulation]] scheme that conveys data by changing (modulating) the amplitude of two carrier waves, 90 degrees out of phase with each other. These two modulated waves are combined and transmitted. [[QAM]] is commonly used in digital wireless communication systems and for high-speed data transmission over limited bandwidth.
